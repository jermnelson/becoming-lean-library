Becoming a Lean Library Chapter 5

Innovation Accounting and Francis Taylor

Abstract
Eric Ries, the originator and founder of the lean startup movement, stresses the importance of what he calls innovation accounting. Innovation accounting is an accounting philosophy originally conceived for use by entrepreneurs in technology startups that diverges from traditional accrual-based accounting used in corporate and nonprofit organizations. While it does not ignore regulatory and administrative requirements provided by traditional accounting systems, innovation accounting encourages the identification and measuring of metrics that provide relevant feedback to the library systems delivering services or resources.

The types and methods of gathering statistics from library services and resources is reminiscent of ideas and philosophies behind one of the twentieth century's most influential management thinkers and educators, Francis Taylor. Taylor attempted to scientifically optimize the workflows of a typical laborer in a factory. Taylor's ideas have had a large impact on library systems' organization and processes, which, with some modification, can be implemented by a lean library using a variant of innovation accounting that is especially adapted to the needs and circumstances of libraries
